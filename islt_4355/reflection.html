Reflection

University Experience

During my four years at the University of Missouri, I have learned a lot about Engineering. During my freshman year of College, I was an Undeclared Engineer. I did not know what type of Engineering I wanted to pursue. I joined an Engineering 1000 course, which discussed all the types of Engineering majors. I found this class very useful to find what I did or did not enjoy. From this class, I found that I enjoyed Industrial and Systems Engineering. Before College, I was not familiar with this type of Engineering. Industrial and Systems Engineering describes how any system functions. This could be in manufacturing, healthcare, commercial, etc. Additionally, data analytics plays a key role in this major. I found that I enjoy probability and statistics, which both play a critical role in systems. From my education at the University of Missouri, I am confident in my abilities for the workplace.

In addition to learning about Engineering at the University of Missouri, I have also formed many relationships. During my freshman year, I joined a Freshman Interest Group (FIG). In the FIG, I lived in the same building as other Engineering students. We had many of the same generic classes and Engineering classes. This allowed us to work together and help each other. I found this valuable in my classes. In addition to this, I participated in Undergraduate Research. I worked in a team with three other students and two professors. This further improved my teamwork skills by collaborating with teammates. By collaborating with teammates, we were able to complete work on time. The relationships I formed at the University of Missouri help me work better in teams, which will help in the workplace.

Work Experience

My internship at Boeing Defense, Space, and Security provided me with industry experience in Industrial Engineering. Boeing is an industry leader in producing products in the commercial, defense, and space sectors. During my internship, I applied my Industrial Engineering education to complete projects. I used my skills in forming relationships to better work in teams. I had a great experience working for Boeing Defense, Space, and Security. I look forward to returning after I graduate.

In the future, I want to be an Engineering Manager. Specifically, I want to manage people on Engineering projects. I enjoy the idea of influencing an Engineering project. This is because I want to manage a project that makes a difference in the world. By starting my career with Boeing Defense, Space, and Security, I will be able to achieve my career goals.
